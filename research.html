Research

Overview

My research examines how institutional structures shape minoritized students’ experiences in education and how systemic barriers can be lowered so students can thrive while bringing their full identities into school spaces. Across my projects, I focus on how cultural development can be celebrated, sustained, and supported through policy, practice, and the everyday environments students move through. My work centers Korean American and immigrant-origin communities and uses qualitative inquiry to understand identity, belonging, and access.

Research Areas

Current and prior projects

Dissertation Research
Higher education | food | belonging | Korean American students
  • Examines the relationship between comfort or familiar food and Korean American students’ sense of belonging on campus.
  • Focuses on how food access and cultural familiarity shape students’ interpretations of care, inclusion, and campus climate.
Independent Research Project (Doctoral Program)
K–12 | language access | policy communication
  • Investigated how K–12 school district websites in California share information about interpretation and translation services.
  • Focused on accessibility, visibility, and how language access information is communicated to families.
Master’s Project (Northwestern University)
Identity | racism | COVID | Korean American generational status
  • Explored Korean American students’ experiences with racism during COVID.
  • Examined self-identification as first-, 1.5-, or second-generation Korean American and how generational positioning shaped identity and belonging.
